Off-peak EV charging

EV charging can make time-of-use rates better or worse depending on when it happens.

In Utah, overnight charging can line up well with off-peak TOU pricing. Charging during weekday evenings can erase the same advantage.

Why EV charging is different from many home loads

EV charging is often flexible. If the vehicle is parked overnight, charging can usually be scheduled after the evening on-peak period. That makes EV load a strong candidate for off-peak savings under a time-of-use rate.

How to model it

  • Load your current interval usage so the calculator can estimate your existing standard and TOU bills.
  • Set the number of future EVs, annual miles, and miles per kWh.
  • Adjust the off-peak charging percentage to compare overnight charging with less disciplined charging habits.

What to watch

If your existing home usage is already expensive under TOU, EV savings may not offset that penalty. If your existing usage is close to neutral, high off-peak EV charging can be enough to make the TOU option look better.

The EV scenario is an energy-only planning estimate. Actual bills depend on the current tariff, riders, taxes, equipment behavior, and how consistently charging stays off-peak.
