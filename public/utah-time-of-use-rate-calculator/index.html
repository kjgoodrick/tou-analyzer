What the calculator answers

A time-of-use plan can be cheaper when most usage happens outside the higher-cost evening window. It can be more expensive when a home has a large share of weekday evening usage. Off Peak Advisor compares those outcomes month by month using interval usage data.

What you need

  • A CSV or Parquet file with interval electricity usage.
  • Your residence type, service phase, and local tax assumptions from your bill.
  • Optional EV assumptions if you want to model future off-peak charging.

How to interpret the result

The headline comparison shows estimated standard and TOU costs for the loaded period. If TOU is close, review the monthly charts and the on-peak share before deciding whether shifting laundry, cooling, cooking, or EV charging could make a meaningful difference.

Rate schedules and rider adjustments can change. Use the calculator as a planning tool and compare its assumptions against your current bill before making a rate decision.
