Schedule 1 TOU

Utah time-of-use rates reward homes that can avoid weekday evening usage.

Schedule 1 TOU separates residential electricity into on-peak and off-peak periods. The key question is how much of your usage lands in the higher-cost evening window.

Analyze your usage Calculator guide

On-peak

The current calculator treats 6 p.m. to 10 p.m. on non-holiday weekdays as the higher-cost TOU window.

Off-peak

All other hours are modeled as off-peak, including weekends, recognized holidays, and overnight periods.

Seasonal rates

Costs can vary by season and rider assumptions, so a month-by-month comparison is more useful than a single average rate.

Why timing matters

A standard residential rate mainly depends on total monthly kilowatt-hours. A time-of-use rate also depends on when those kilowatt-hours happen. Two homes can use the same annual energy and see different results if one uses more power during weekday evenings.

Loads that can affect the result

  • Air conditioning during summer evenings.
  • Electric cooking, laundry, and dishwashing between 6 p.m. and 10 p.m.
  • Water heating and space heating if they run during on-peak hours.
  • EV charging, especially if it can be scheduled overnight.

How Off Peak Advisor models the comparison

The app reads interval usage, classifies each interval as on-peak or off-peak, then estimates standard and TOU bills with current built-in assumptions. The charts show whether savings are steady across the year or concentrated in specific months.
